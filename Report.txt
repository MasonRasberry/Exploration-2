Generally, the hashing method will be faster than the brute force in most cases. Though due to the need to create an object, hashing is not always faster for small arrays.
When input sizes increases, the lower time O(2n) complexity of the hashing method makes it much faster at scale than the O(n^2) time complexity of the brute force method.
As for space complexity, the brute force method uses O(n) space because it only needs one array, the input array. The hashing method on the other hand uses both an input array and a hash map of equal size, giving it a space complexity of O(n^2).
Despite that, the speed difference between the two methods makes the space tradeoff well worth the time gained in computation speed.
